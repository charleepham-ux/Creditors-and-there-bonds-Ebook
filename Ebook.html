PART ONE: FOUNDATION — THE WORLDVIEW OF THIS SYSTEM

1.1 | The Central Thesis (What This Book Is Really About)

This work presents a specific legal/commercial theory about the relationship between:

  • You (a living human being)
  • Your "straw man" (a legal fiction entity created when you were born, identified by your ALL-CAPS name)
  • The government / courts / agencies (treated here as corporate entities in bankruptcy)

The Core Argument in Plain English:

The United States has been in a form of bankruptcy since 1933. Because there is no longer real money (gold/silver), the entire system runs on credit — specifically, YOUR credit. You are described as the TRUE CREDITOR. When any government entity (IRS, court, city, county) sends you a bill, charge, ticket, or lawsuit, it is really asking to USE your credit. If you know this, you can "accept" instead of "fight," become the creditor in any proceeding, and discharge obligations through your "exemption" rather than paying with money.

Why This Matters:

  • People who "fight" in court LOSE because they become debtors
  • People who accept and discharge become creditors — and creditors always win
  • Every presentment (charge, bill, ticket) is actually an opportunity, not a threat

1.2 | The 1933 Turning Point — Why "There Is No Money"

This is the foundational historical claim everything builds on.

What Happened:

  • Before 1933: U.S. had gold-backed money; people could hold gold coins
  • April–June 1933: President Roosevelt ordered all gold turned in to Federal Reserve Banks
  • May 1, 1933 deadline: Gold deposited in exchange for Federal Reserve Notes (paper)
  • June 1933: House Joint Resolution 192 — Congress declared that no creditor could demand payment in gold; all debts could be discharged with Federal Reserve Notes

Congressional Record:

Congressman Patman stated on the floor of the 73rd Congress that the new money "will represent a mortgage on all the homes and other property of all the people in the Nation" and "will not have one penny of gold coverage behind it."

Interpretation:

Old System (Pre-1933)New System (Post-1933)
Real money = gold/silver coin"Money" = debt instruments (FRNs)
You OWN your property by payingYou get POSSESSION but not title
Government had to pay goldGovernment discharges with credit
People were private and sovereignPeople's credit backs the whole system

Key Principle Established:

"There is no money." Federal Reserve Notes are evidence of DEBT, not payment. When you use them, you are exchanging one debt for another. The only way to truly discharge (eliminate) a debt is with CREDIT from the private side — which is YOUR exemption.

1.4 | The Straw Man — Explained Completely

What It Is

A straw man (also written "strawman") is a legal fiction entity — essentially a paper corporation — that was created when your mother applied for your birth certificate.

Definition (Black's Law Dictionary):

A "front"; a third party who is put up in name only to take part in a transaction. A nominal party to a transaction; one (JOHN) who acts as an agent for another (John) for the purposes of taking title to real property and executing whatever documents and instruments the principal (John) may direct.

How It Was Created

  1. Your mother applied to the State for a birth certificate
  2. She provided a physical description (weight, height, footprint)
  3. The State created the straw man entity using the ALL-CAPS version of your name
  4. The State applied for a Social Security Number for it
  5. The straw man was enrolled in commerce: schools, medical records, licenses — all in its name

The Trust Structure of the Straw Man

Your birth certificate created a trust with three parties:

BeneficiaryEither YOU (if you claim it) or the State (if you don't)

Important: If you never claim your beneficial interest, the State uses the straw man's assets and credit for its own purposes.

What the Straw Man Owns

Everything registered with the State belongs, in this framework, to the straw man (not to you):

  • Car titles
  • Real estate deeds (if mortgaged)
  • Business licenses
  • Driver's licenses
  • Bank accounts with Social Security-linked numbers

Key Claim:

"None of those assets belong to you. They are all pieces of paper that belong to the straw man, UNLESS it fails to follow the rules."

1.5 | The Exemption — Your Unlimited Credit

This is the mechanism that can discharge any public debt.

What It Is:

Your exemption is the credit you have as the private creditor who backed the U.S. system by pledging their substance (through the birth certificate, Social Security, car titles, mortgages, etc.). Because the U.S. owes a debt to the people who funded it, you have an infinite line of credit to offset public charges.

Why It's Unlimited:

Since ALL substance in the country is pledged to back the system, your exemption has no dollar ceiling — it expands to cover whatever obligation needs discharging.

How It Works:

  1. Government creates a charge against your straw man (e.g., $5,000 fine)
  2. That charge is a PUBLIC DEBT — a liability on the government's books
  3. Your EXEMPTION is the ASSET that can offset it
  4. When you "accept for value" and authorize your exemption, you provide the credit
  5. The books balance; the charge is discharged; the case ends

Analogy:

Imagine you are the silent investor (private creditor) who funded a company (the U.S.). The company runs a store (the court system). When the store creates an invoice for your account-holder (straw man), you — as the investor — can simply write off the invoice from your investment account. The store HAS to accept it because you're the one who funded the whole operation.

PART TWO: THE RULES OF THE GAME

2.1 | The Four Rules

Four rules govern everything in this framework:

RULE #1: The Fiction and the Real Cannot Mix

Public and Private Are Separate Worlds

  • Fictions (straw men, corporations, public entities) cannot interact with substance (real people, gold, private property) without creating a jurisdictional problem
  • You cannot use public routing numbers on private instruments
  • You cannot create a public debt from the private side
  • You ARE a private banker with your own private routing number (your EIN)

RULE #2: Stay in Honor at All Costs

Honor = compliance with the commercial process; Dishonor = fighting/arguing/ignoring

This is the most operationally important rule.

HONOR includes:

  • Accepting a presentment
  • Returning it with authorization to use your credit
  • Not disputing the facts
  • Not arguing with judge, attorney, or process

DISHONOR includes:

  • Defending yourself
  • Filing motions
  • Pleading "not guilty"
  • Testifying
  • Arguing
  • Ignoring a presentment
  • Calling witnesses to fight the case
  • Questioning whether the State has a right to make its claim

Why This Is Critical:

"Whoever bonds the case is the creditor. Whoever is in dishonor is the debtor. They need you to dishonor the process, the attorneys, or the judge to have the standard script result in the standard outcome."

When you dishonor, you GIVE the prosecuting attorney the commercial energy to bond the case — and take your credit. When you stay in honor, the energy stays with you.

The "Hot Potato" Analogy:


Prosecuting attorney starts with liability
   → Defense attorney takes it (if they file)
      → Straw man takes it (if you volunteer to represent it)
         → YOU take it (if you fight, testify, or argue)

If you ACCEPT for value, the liability stays with the prosecuting attorney.

RULE #3: There Is No Money

FRNs are debt instruments, not payment

  • Federal Reserve Notes are LIABILITIES of the Federal Reserve
  • Using them "pays" a debt only on the public side by exchanging one debt for another
  • You can never get TITLE to anything with FRNs — only possession
  • The straw man can USE FRNs; you (the private man) cannot pay debts with them
  • Your only real asset is your EXEMPTION (credit from the private side)

RULE #5: Do Not Participate in Public Plays

(Note: Rule #4 is intentionally absent from the numbering)

What This Means:

When you get a charge, summons, or ticket, understand it as a "play" — a scripted performance with assigned roles. The system NEEDS you to volunteer as the trustee for the straw man to execute the script.

The Default Script:

  1. Straw man is charged
  2. You volunteer to represent it (trustee)
  3. Defense attorney defends (you hire one)
  4. Case proceeds — you're treated as debtor
  5. You lose, pay, or go to jail

When You Don't Participate:

  1. The script breaks
  2. The prosecuting attorney has no one to pass liability to
  3. You step in as private creditor
  4. You accept and discharge on the private side
  5. No controversy exists — case collapses or settles

2.2 | Why Courts Always Seem to Win

Modern courts are NOT courts of law but equity/admiralty courts operating on presumptions. The key hidden presumptions:

  1. You are the same person as your straw man (the "union" presumption)
  2. You have voluntarily entered the public commercial system
  3. Your silence or argument = consent to their jurisdiction
  4. You are a DEBTOR until you prove otherwise

How These Presumptions Work:

A presumption is not a fact — it is an ASSUMPTION the court treats as true unless rebutted. These presumptions:

  • Are never announced in court
  • Cannot be challenged by arguing "law" or "facts" (that's traversing — dishonor)
  • Can ONLY be rebutted by placing documented evidence on the record

Key Legal Concept — Traversing:

"Traverser. In pleading, one who traverses or denies. A prisoner or party indicted; so called from his traversing the indictment." — Black's Law Dictionary

When you traverse (deny the charges), you have:

  • Joined the controversy
  • Confirmed jurisdiction
  • Made the imaginary dispute real
  • Become bound by the outcome

The Catch-22:

  • If you argue → dishonor → you lose
  • If you ignore → also dishonor → you lose
  • The ONLY exit: Accept for value from the private side → you stay in honor → creditor wins

PART THREE: THE BONDS — ALL SIX TYPES

3.1 | What Is a Bond?

Technical Definition:

A bond is always evidence of a debt. It can be a liability to the debtor OR an asset to the creditor — depending on your position.

Plain English:

A bond is a written document that proves a debt relationship exists. When YOU issue a bond, you are the creditor. When the COURT issues a bond, it proves the debt they need to settle.

Why Every Case Needs a Bond:

Courts cannot hear a case without a bond. Someone must put up commercial security — evidence of a debt — for the court to have jurisdiction (technically: for the ledger to balance). If there is no bond in the clerk's file, YOU can bond the case and become the creditor.

3.2 | Bond #1 — The Bond Supporting Credit Authorizations

What It Is:

The foundational implied bond that exists because:

  • Your grandparents turned in gold in 1933
  • You were issued a birth certificate (pledging your description as collateral)
  • You've applied for car titles, mortgages, Social Security, and other state-registered items

Plain English:

This bond is the implicit IOU the U.S. government owes YOU for all the times you pledged substance to keep the system running. It is the source of your "credit" on the private side.

How It Works:

When you "pledge" something to the public side (get a license, register a car, take out a mortgage), you receive an implied bond in return — evidence that the system owes you credit.

Key Phrase to Remember:

"A bond is always evidence of a debt — it can be a liability to the debtor or an asset to the creditor."

3.3 | Bond #2 — The Bond for Discharge

What It Is:

This is the practical use of your credit. When you USE a bond to discharge a public debt, you are using the credit that is owed to you to offset (cancel out) a charge on the public side.

How It Works:

  • A charge is created on the public books (e.g., $5,000 fine)
  • This charge is a DEBIT on one side of the ledger
  • Your exemption (bond) provides the CREDIT on the other side
  • The books balance → debt is discharged

Important Clarification:

"There is nothing that is going to be transferred from the U.S. Treasury to the holder's bank. There is no funds transfer. There is no money transfer. There is no credit transfer. The credits are in your instrument with your signature on it."

The signature on your instrument IS the value. Your autograph as the private creditor is the asset that closes the accounting.

3.4 | Bond #3 — The Appearance Bond

What It Is:

A bond that guarantees you will appear in court. You are ENTITLED to an appearance bond (at no cost to you) if you stay in honor.

How to Get It:

  • Accept the charging instrument for value
  • Return it with authorization to use your credit
  • Tell the judge you are not disputing the facts
  • Request an appearance bond at no cost to you
  • There must be NO controversy (no dispute, no arguing)

Appearance Bond vs. Bail Bond:

Critical Warning:

"If you don't appear AND settle the accounting, you will be in dishonor of your word (your bond), and the appearance bond will be revoked. They will not tell you it has been revoked."

Once you accept the appearance bond, you MUST follow through with settling the account. If you default, the straw man's debt falls on you.

3.5 | Bond #4 — The Surety Bond

What It Is:

A bond that transfers (subrogates) liability from one party to another. You issue a surety bond when you want to:

  1. Relieve someone else of financial liability
  2. Indemnify an honorable party who made a mistake
  3. Protect yourself from a judge or officer who might dishonor you

Three Parties Required:

  1. The Surety = you (volunteering to guarantee)
  2. The Debtor = the party whose liability you are taking
  3. The Creditor = the party who is owed

When to Use It:

  • An official has dishonored you
  • You want to protect an honorable judge who is doing their job
  • You need to create a record of dishonor before escalating

Important Rules:

  • You must have EVIDENCE of dishonor before issuing
  • You must have "clean hands" (not be in dishonor yourself)
  • Deliver it to: the dishonoring party + their bonding company + the clerk of court
  • Always get a certified copy after filing

Key Principle:

"Suretyship: The relationship among three parties whereby one person (the surety) guarantees payment of debtor's debt owed to a creditor or acts as a co-debtor."

3.6 | Bond #5 — The Case Bond

What It Is:

A bond you file with the court to BOND THE CASE. When you bond the case, YOU become the creditor in the proceeding.

Historical Background:

In common law, a replevin bond was required before anyone could bring a case to court. The moving party had to bond their charges — put up security — before temporarily seizing disputed property. Modern courts have abandoned requiring this, but the principle remains.

Why There Is Usually No Bond in the File:

"Charges are rarely bonded in modern court procedures, until after the case is decided. By that time, the defendant is almost always in dishonor, so the prosecuting attorney can use the defendant's dishonor to bond the case."

This means: the system relies on YOUR dishonor as the bond. When you fight, argue, or testify, you provide the energy that bonds the case — against yourself.

How to Bond the Case:

  1. Check the clerk's file — get a certified copy of the docket showing NO bond exists
  2. Prepare your bond (see Sample #8)
  3. File it with the clerk
  4. You are now the creditor → creditors win
  5. Attach a photocopy of the docket sheet to prove no prior bond existed

Critical Rule:

Once you bond the case and become the creditor, you CANNOT dishonor anyone or anything. If you do, you lose your creditor position and revert to representing the straw man.

3.7 | Bond #6 — The Performance Bond

What It Is:

A bond that guarantees an officer or fiduciary will faithfully perform their duties. All public officials have performance bonds (backed by insurance companies). An oath of office IS a performance bond.

Why It Matters:

  • Every judge has a bond
  • Every prosecutor has a bond (their bar number backs it)
  • These bonds can be made commercially liable if the officer acts in dishonor

Key Concept: Accepting the Judge's Oath and Bond for Value

One of the steps in the court process (Step #4) is to:

  1. Obtain a certified copy of the judge's oath of office
  2. Accept it for value
  3. This creates a private contract between you and the judge

When you accept the judge's oath for value, you are establishing a contract: the judge must treat you as a creditor and act in honor. If they don't, their bond (and their bonding company) can be held liable.

Key Principle:

"The reason you are bringing the bond into this issue is to notify risk management if necessary that you have been damaged by one of its insured persons."

PART FOUR: THE ADMINISTRATIVE PROCESS

4.1 | Why You Need an Administrative Process FIRST

Before any court case, you must complete an administrative process. This creates:

  • A formal record of your acceptance and return
  • A record of the other party's dishonor (if they don't respond)
  • A Certificate of Non-Response — a "judgment in estoppel"
  • Irrefutable evidence you can bring into any court

Judgment in Estoppel:

When someone fails to respond to a properly served notice, they have tacitly AGREED to everything stated in the notice. A notary certifies this. This becomes unimpeachable evidence that:

  • You accepted
  • They failed to honor your acceptance
  • The account is settled
  • They have no capacity to pursue further collection

4.2 | The Key Player: The Notary

The notary is a pivotal figure — more powerful than a judge in certain ways.

Why the Notary Is So Important:

  • A notary's log is a public commercial record
  • It is admissible as evidence in ANY court
  • A notary is a neutral third-party witness
  • A notary's certificate is prima facie evidence of the facts it states
  • Historically, notaries "wrote the king's papers" and issued writs
  • A public notary can place evidence into a court record that cannot be refused

What the Notary Does for You:

  1. Mails your Notice of Acceptance (as the sender — keeping you private)
  2. Gives you a Certificate of Service (proof of mailing)
  3. If no response: sends a Notice of Non-Response
  4. Issues a Certificate of Non-Response (your judgment in estoppel)

Important Warning:

"DO NOT PUT YOUR NOTARIES IN JEOPARDY!!"

Never ask a notary to make legal determinations. Their job is to certify facts and actions, not render legal opinions.

4.3 | The Four-Step Administrative Process

STEP 1: VISUALIZATION — Know What You Want Before You Start

Before doing anything, decide:

  • What outcome do you want from the case?
  • What do you want the judge to do?
  • What evidence will you need?
  • What should the Certificate of Non-Response say?

"Put the horse before the cart." The wording of your Certificate cannot be changed after it's issued. Plan it before you start.

STEP 2: PREPARE THE CERTIFICATE OF NON-RESPONSE

This is the notary's certificate, not yours. It will be issued AFTER the administrative process is complete.

What It Must Establish:

  • a) You accepted and returned the presentment
  • b) You exchanged your exemption for a discharge
  • c) Your acceptance was received (twice)
  • d) The respondent refused to respond
  • e) There is an agreement of the parties (by silence)
  • f) The respondent has no commercial energy to pursue collection
  • g) You have all the commercial energy (you are in honor)
  • h) The respondent is in dishonor

Format Requirements:

  • Name of notary
  • Name of presenter (the charging party)
  • Name of accepter (you)
  • Description of the presentment
  • Reference to state commercial statute (UCC 1-202 equivalent)
  • Certificate statement of all facts above
  • Notary signature, seal, stamp, address, administrative process number

STEP 3: PREPARE YOUR NOTICE OF ACCEPTANCE

This is YOUR letter to the charging party (cover letter to your returned presentment).

What It Must Say:

  • You have accepted the presentment for assessed value
  • You are returning it in exchange for closure and settlement
  • Please send confirmation the account is adjusted and settled, OR send a notice of dishonor from a qualified third party
  • You are enclosing an authorization to use your credit (bill of exchange or equivalent)
  • Failure to respond will be their agreement that they have no capacity to pursue collection
  • Further collection confirms their agreement to owe YOU a stated amount

Mailing:

The notary mails this, not you. You stay on the private side. The return address is the notary's address. Sent by certified mail, return receipt requested.

STEP 4: PREPARE THE NOTICE OF NON-RESPONSE FOR THE NOTARY

This is the notary's SECOND notice — sent if no response is received in 10 days.

What It Does:

  • Informs the respondent their non-response was noticed
  • Attaches a copy of the original presentment
  • Makes a second request
  • States that failure to cure within 10 days = agreement to all prior statements

The 10-Day Rule:

Based on Regulation Z (Federal Truth in Lending, 15 USC 1601):

  • 3 days for mailing
  • 3 days for the respondent to decide
  • 3 days for return mail
  • 1 day for service (not counted)

= 10 days total

PART FIVE: THE COURT PROCESS — STEP BY STEP

5.1 | The 15-Step Court Process

Here is the complete process in plain English:

STEP 1: Accept and Return the Court Presentment

Write on the charging document:

  • If it has an assessed value: "Accepted for assessed value and returned in exchange for closure and settlement of this accounting."
  • If no assessed value: "Accepted for value and returned in exchange for closure and settlement of this accounting."
  • Add: Date / Your signature / Your EIN (no dashes)

STEP 2: Attach a Bill of Exchange (Credit Authorization)

This is the instrument that authorizes the State to use your credit. A Bill of Exchange must include:

  • Date and account number
  • Dollar value (the amount of the charge)
  • Name of who receives the credits
  • Name of public creditor (Secretary of the Treasury)
  • Name of public pass-through (your straw man)
  • Instructions
  • Your name (as private creditor)
  • Your exemption number
  • Your signature (the endorsement)

Important: Keep the instructions in plain English. This is where the Treasury Tax and Loan (TT&L) process is referenced. The Secretary of the Treasury must be notified that you are using your exemption.

STEP 3: Send a Letter of Credit to the Treasury

The Secretary of the Treasury must be notified when you use your exemption. Without this step, it's like writing a check on an account with no funds. The Treasury (the public auditor) must receive notice of your intent to use your exemption for the discharge.

STEP 4: Get Certified Copy of Judge's Oath → Accept for Value

  • Obtain from: Secretary of State, County Recorder, or court clerk
  • Accept it for value
  • This creates your private contract with the judge
  • File a copy with the clerk (gives notice of your contract)
  • Introduce the original into the court's evidence file in open court

STEP 5: Get Certified Copy of Judge's Bond → Accept for Value

  • The judge's bond covers their public liability
  • Accepting it notifies their bonding company / risk management
  • If the judge acts in dishonor, this creates a commercial liability trail
  • Note: This binds the JUDGE position, not necessarily the man personally — though private liability may extend to the man if he treats you as a private party

STEP 6: Private Mailing to the Judge

Send a private letter (via your notary, certified mail RRR, marked "Private") to the judge personally — NOT for filing with the clerk.

What the Letter Contains:

  • You have accepted and returned the presentment for value
  • You exchanged your exemption for discharge of the charges
  • You want settlement and closure
  • You are requesting an appearance bond at no cost to you
  • You are NOT disputing the facts
  • The parties have reached an agreement (attach certificate)
  • You have accepted the judge's oath and bond for value

STEP 7: File a Public Notice of Acceptance

This is a court filing that lets the public record show you are doing something on the private side. If you don't do this, it looks like you're ignoring the presentment (dishonor → default judgment).

What It Says:

  • You intend to settle and close the accounting immediately
  • You have accepted and returned the presentment
  • You exchanged your exemption for discharge
  • You are requesting an appearance bond at no cost
  • You do not dispute the facts
  • You will enter a plea for the Defendant
  • You want to know when to appear to receive the bond and enter the plea

STEP 8: Get Court Order Appointing Public Defender → Accept for Value

If the court appoints a public defender:

  • Accept the appointment for value
  • Return it to the attorney with instructions
  • The public defender now works for YOU, not as a defense attorney

Important Distinction:

The public defender is NOT defending the straw man. They are acting as your counsel — your mouthpiece — to read your documents into the record and negotiate settlement.

STEP 9: Prepare Letter of Instructions to the Attorney (The Lawyer Letter)

This letter IS your contract with the attorney. Without establishing terms, they will default to defending the straw man as an officer of the court.

Key Instructions to Include:

  1. You are intervening — your interest may not be protected by existing parties
  2. Accept the appointment for value and return it
  3. Request the attorney ESCROW their BAR card during this case
  4. Act as COUNSEL, not as attorney
  5. Do NOT argue any facts or public issues
  6. Do NOT foster or join any argument
  7. Do NOT defend the Defendant
  8. Read the entire letter into the record in open court
  9. File it with the clerk

PART SIX: KEY CONCEPTS EXPLAINED SIMPLY

6.1 | Legal Fictions and Why the System Is Built on Them

The legal fiction concept — drawn from Lon L. Fuller's Legal Fictions (1967) — explains why the system works the way it does.

What Is a Legal Fiction?

A legal fiction is a statement that everyone knows is false but that the legal system treats as true for practical purposes. Example: the All-Caps Name (JOHN HENRY DOE) is "you" for legal purposes even though it's really a corporate entity separate from you.

Why Fictions Matter:

  • The entire court system operates on fictions and presumptions
  • These fictions only BECOME REAL when you agree to them (by traversing or arguing)
  • Your silence, acceptance, or dishonor all ACTIVATE the fictions
  • By staying private and accepting for value, you refuse to activate the fiction

Key Metaphor:

The legal system is like Alice in Wonderland — "Alice in Wonderland was written as a satire on the legal system, where things are an ever-changing mirage and nothing is as it appears."

6.2 | Presentments — What They REALLY Are

Plain English Definition:

A presentment is any document sent to you that creates an obligation — a bill, ticket, summons, complaint, indictment, IRS notice, or court order.

The Correct Framing:

Most people see a presentment as a THREAT. Here is the correct way to see it:

  1. A request from the State to use your credit
  2. An opportunity to establish yourself as the creditor
  3. A gift — if handled correctly

What Happens If You Fight/Ignore:

6.4 | The Concept of Honor and Dishonor

This is a commercial, not a moral, concept.

Honor (Commercial Definition):

Accepting a bill/presentment and following through with the expected commercial response (endorsement, return, credit authorization).

Dishonor (Commercial Definition):

Refusing, arguing, ignoring, or otherwise failing to respond to a commercial instrument as expected.

Why "Dishonor" Has Consequences:

In commercial law (UCC), dishonor of a negotiable instrument creates immediate consequences:

  • The dishonoring party bears all liability
  • The notary can certify the dishonor
  • The dishonor becomes the "bond" that energizes further collection

Key Principle:

"Your voluntary dishonor will authorize the use of your credit to bond the case. Since you did not voluntarily bond the case, you are in dishonor."

6.5 | The Banker's Acceptance (BA)

What It Is:

A Banker's Acceptance is a negotiable instrument where a bank (or in this case, you as a private banker) accepts and endorses a draft, taking on the obligation to pay.

PART EIGHT: VOCABULARY AND DEFINITIONS MASTER LIST

8.1 | Core Vocabulary (Simplified + Technical)

ACCEPTANCE FOR VALUE

  • Simplified: Endorsing a charge and using your credit to discharge it instead of fighting it
  • Technical: A Banker's Acceptance performed by the private creditor, converting a public debt obligation into a discharged account
  • Example: Writing "Accepted for value" on a traffic ticket and returning it with a Bill of Exchange

ADMIRALTY / MARITIME LAW

  • Simplified: Commercial law originally governing ships and sea contracts — now applied to ALL U.S. courts
  • Technical: International commercial jurisdiction operating on presumption of contract; no common law rights apply
  • Why It Matters: In admiralty, you're presumed guilty unless you prove innocence; the burden of proof is on the defendant

APPEARANCE BOND

  • Simplified: A guarantee (backed by your word/credit) that you'll show up to settle the case — issued at no cost if you stay in honor
  • Technical: A bond securing the presence of the surety/creditor for accounting resolution, issued by the hearing officer when no controversy exists

BAIL BOND

  • Simplified: What the straw man (debtor) needs to get out of jail — costs money and comes with conditions
  • Technical: Financial surety posted by a debtor entity to secure temporary freedom pending adjudication

BANKER'S ACCEPTANCE (BA)

  • Simplified: When you endorse a draft (charge/bill) and take on the obligation — but discharge it with your private credit, not cash
  • Technical: A negotiable instrument accepted by a private banker (you) under UCC commercial law, creating offset and adjustment

BENEFICIARY

  • Simplified: The party who benefits from a trust's assets
  • Technical: One who benefits from another's act; holds equitable title to trust corpus

BILL OF EXCHANGE

  • Simplified: A written instruction to a bank/treasury to pay a certain amount — you issue these to authorize use of your credit
  • Technical: A written, unconditional order by one party (drawer) directing another (drawee) to pay a sum to a third party (payee)

BOND

  • Simplified: Written evidence of a debt — can be a liability to the debtor or an asset to the creditor
  • Technical: In every case a bond represents debt; its holder is a creditor of the corporation

CASE BOND

  • Simplified: Your bond filed with the court to make yourself the creditor in the case
  • Technical: A bond in the nature of a replevin bond filed in the court record to provide subject matter jurisdiction and establish the creditor

CERTIFICATE OF NON-RESPONSE

  • Simplified: A notary's official document certifying that someone failed to respond to your acceptance — this IS your judgment
  • Technical: A notarial certificate establishing dishonor and creating a judgment in estoppel on the commercial record

CHARGING INSTRUMENT

  • Simplified: Any document that creates a charge against your straw man: ticket, bill, complaint, indictment, summons
  • Technical: A public-side instrument creating a liability on the commercial ledger that requires an offsetting asset to discharge

DISHONOR

  • Simplified: Failing to respond properly to a commercial instrument — includes arguing, ignoring, or fighting
  • Technical: Under UCC, failure to accept or pay a negotiable instrument when due; creates commercial liability in the dishonoring party

DISCHARGE

  • Simplified: Eliminating (not "paying") a debt by providing the offsetting credit entry on the books
  • Technical: Balancing the public-side debit (charge) with a private-side credit (exemption) through acceptance for value

EQUITY

  • Simplified: A legal system based on "what is right" — governed by the judge's conscience, not fixed rules
  • Technical: The body of law supplementing common law; administered through courts of chancery; based on fairness rather than strict legal rules

EIN (EXEMPTION IDENTIFICATION NUMBER)

  • Simplified: Your Social Security number WITHOUT the dashes — this is your private creditor identifier
  • Technical: The private routing number correlating to your exemption on the private side of the accounting

ESTOPPEL

  • Simplified: Being legally prevented from changing your position because of a prior action or silence
  • Technical: A doctrine preventing one party from asserting a right or claim inconsistent with a prior position that another party relied upon

EXEMPTION

  • Simplified: Your unlimited line of private credit that can offset any public charge
  • Technical: The private-side credit account representing the living principal's investment in the public system, accessible through acceptance for value

FIDUCIARY

  • Simplified: Someone with a legal duty to manage property for the benefit of another
  • Technical: A person who holds a legal or ethical obligation to act in good faith for another's benefit; trustees have fiduciary duties

HONOR

  • Simplified: Properly accepting and following through with a commercial instrument
  • Technical: Under UCC, accepting a negotiable instrument and performing on it as required by the terms of commercial law

HOUSE JOINT RESOLUTION 192

  • Simplified: The 1933 congressional act that made it legal to pay all debts with Federal Reserve Notes instead of gold
  • Technical: HJR-192, 73rd Congress, June 5, 1933 — abrogated gold clauses in contracts; established FRNs as legal tender for all debts

IN REM JURISDICTION

  • Simplified: Court jurisdiction over property/things (not persons directly)
  • Technical: Power of a court over the res (the thing/property at issue in a case) rather than over the person

JUDGMENT IN ESTOPPEL

  • Simplified: When someone's silence or non-response creates a legal agreement — the notary's certificate certifies this
  • Technical: A judgment barring a party from contesting established facts because they failed to respond or act within the required time

LEGAL FICTION

  • Simplified: A false statement that law treats as true for practical purposes — the straw man is a legal fiction
  • Technical: An assumption that something is true even though it may be false; used by courts to enable actions they could not otherwise perform

NOTARY

  • Simplified: A public official who certifies facts and documents — your most powerful ally in this system
  • Technical: A public officer authorized to certify documents, take oaths, and serve as a bonded neutral third party holding the commercial record

PERFORMANCE BOND

  • Simplified: A guarantee by an official that they will faithfully perform their duties — judges and prosecutors have these
  • Technical: A type of contract bond protecting against loss due to inability or refusal of a person to perform their contractual/official obligations

PRESENTMENT

  • Simplified: Any charge, bill, ticket, or demand sent to you requiring a response
  • Technical: Under UCC, the demand for acceptance or payment of a negotiable instrument; in this context, any document demanding action from the straw man

PRIVATE SIDE

  • Simplified: Your world as a living human — real, sovereign, the source of credit
  • Technical: The substance/private jurisdiction where real beings, gold, common law, and genuine contracts exist; operates on equity and substance

PUBLIC DEFENDER

  • Simplified: Court-appointed attorney — in this system, they become YOUR counsel (not the straw man's attorney) once you instruct them
  • Technical: An attorney appointed by the court to represent the defendant; can be redirected to serve as the living principal's counsel through proper instruction

PUBLIC SIDE

  • Simplified: The world of fictions — straw men, corporations, government agencies, courts, Federal Reserve Notes
  • Technical: The commercial/admiralty/equity jurisdiction; a derivative of the private side; operates on debt, assumptions, and presumptions

RES JUDICATA

  • Simplified: A matter that has already been decided — cannot be relitigated
  • Technical: Latin for "a matter judged"; a final judgment on the merits bars further litigation on the same matter

REPLEVIN BOND

  • Simplified: The old common law requirement to bond your charges before a court would hear your case — modern courts no longer require this upfront, but the principle remains
  • Technical: In common law, a bond required before a court would allow temporary possession of disputed property pending resolution

SET-OFF / OFFSET

  • Simplified: Canceling one debt by applying an equal credit — what you do when you "discharge" a charge with your exemption
  • Technical: The right of a debtor to reduce the amount owed to a creditor by a counterclaim; mutual adjusting of accounts

STRAW MAN

  • Simplified: Your legal fiction alter ego — the ALL-CAPS entity created by your birth certificate
  • Technical: A nominal party to a transaction; a front used to take title or carry out actions for another; the corporate person attached to a living being's name

SUBROGATION

  • Simplified: Stepping into someone else's legal position — taking over their rights AND obligations
  • Technical: The substitution of one party for another in relation to a debt or claim; allows the substitute to assert the original party's rights

SURETY

  • Simplified: The person who guarantees payment if the primary party defaults
  • Technical: A person who is primarily liable for the debt or performance of another; binds themselves to pay another's debt if the principal fails

TRAVERSE

  • Simplified: Denying the charges — arguing, pleading not guilty, defending yourself
  • Technical: In pleading, denying allegations in the opponent's pleading; joining the issues presented; creating a dispute

TRUST

  • Simplified: A legal arrangement where one person (trustee) holds property for the benefit of another (beneficiary)
  • Technical: A legal entity created by a grantor for designated beneficiaries under state law; requires trustor, trustee, and beneficiary

UCC (UNIFORM COMMERCIAL CODE)

  • Simplified: The national rulebook for commercial transactions — this whole system runs under these rules
  • Technical: A comprehensive statutory scheme governing commercial transactions across all U.S. states; includes Article 3 (negotiable instruments) and Article 9 (secured transactions)

9.3 | Common Confusions — Cleared Up

Confusion: "Accepting means I'm admitting guilt."

Accepting the presentment means accepting the INSTRUMENT — the commercial paper — for value. You are not admitting to the statutory interpretation of your acts. You are admitting the FACTS (you wrote a check, you drove a car) but NOT agreeing that those facts constitute a statutory violation or crime. Agreeing to facts ends controversy; pleading not guilty to statutes creates controversy.

Confusion: "The court is bound by law — I can argue the law."

Modern courts are admiralty/equity courts, NOT courts of law. In equity, the judge's conscience governs — there are no "facts" except what the parties stipulate. Arguing law is traversing = dishonor = you lose.

Confusion: "A good lawyer can win this for me."

As soon as a defense attorney files a notice of appearance, they take on liability — but they will defend the straw man, not you. This creates dishonor. Instead, instruct a court-appointed public defender to be your COUNSEL only — to read your letter into the record and request settlement. Never hire an attorney to "defend."

Confusion: "If there's no money, how does anything get paid?"

Obligations are DISCHARGED (balanced on the books) not PAID. Your acceptance for value + bill of exchange creates the bookkeeping entry that offsets the debit. Numbers change in a ledger — nothing physically transfers.

Confusion: "Why would the government accept this?"

The government HAS to accept it — they have no commercial capacity without your credit. The U.S. is in bankruptcy; it cannot make valid claims. When you supply your credit, you give them what they need. Refusing to accept (by fighting) forces them to take your credit anyway — through your dishonor.
