How to Use This System

This is a complete, exam-focused breakdown of Creditors and Their Bonds, Plus the Hidden Commercial Court Process. It assumes you are starting from scratch. Everything you need to pass a fast, high-pressure quiz on this material is here — no summary required first, just testable facts, definitions, distinctions, and traps.

The fastest path to a passing score: (1) Read the Cram Sheet twice. (2) Drill the 100 Most Likely questions until you can answer each in under 8 seconds. (3) Run Rapid Fire for reflex speed. (4) Use Flashcards the night before. (5) Use the full Question Bank to find and close your weak spots.

Difficulty levels are tagged on every question: L1 Easy recognition · L2 Basic understanding · L3 Apply · L4 Advanced · L5 Expert. Each question has a hidden answer + explanation + why the wrong options fail. Tap an option to test yourself, or tap Show answer.

The five things an instructor will test to see if you really understand: (1) The public/private (fiction vs. real) distinction and why they can never mix. (2) The difference between discharging a debt and paying a debt. (3) Honor vs. dishonor and what triggers a default judgment. (4) The three jurisdictions (Common Law, Equity, Admiralty) and how "colorable"/Statutory jurisdiction hides Admiralty. (5) The strawman / trust structure created by the birth certificate, and who plays Creator, Trustee, and Beneficiary.

Study aid only. This material summarizes the claims, terminology, and theories asserted within the source text for examination-preparation purposes. Many of these claims — about money, jurisdiction, citizenship, and the legal effect of these procedures — are legal theories advanced by the "redemption"/sovereign-citizen movement and are not recognized as valid by U.S. courts; "strawman," "accepted for value," and similar arguments have been consistently rejected and can carry real legal penalties. This guide does not endorse the theories and is not legal advice. It exists to help you answer questions about this material.
